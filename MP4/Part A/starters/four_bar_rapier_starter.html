Notes
The reference position is θin = θmin; displacement is measured from the finger tip there. For the defaults, θmin=0° is the max-open state and θmax=50° is closed — displacement grows as the gripper closes.
Why O₂ is offset from the mirror axis. Your side's input pivot O₂ is the gear pivot for the gear pair that synchronizes both sides. Each gear has its own center; the two gears mesh at the gripper centerline (the mirror axis). So O₂'s X coordinate = half the gear pair center distance. Default O₂=(8, 0) with mirror at x=0 → 16 mm gear pair center distance, each gear roughly 8 mm radius. You'll design the actual gear pair in Part B; for now just pick an offset that leaves space for a printable gear.
The defaults use a parallelogram four-bar (L1=L3, L2=L4) matching the BigClaw's parallel-gripping mechanism. O₂=(8,0) and O₄=(8,12) give a vertical ground link (φ=90°), so the coupler stays vertical at every θ — the finger translates without rotating. The dead-center is at θ=90°; the starter range 0°–50° stays safely below this, giving transmission angles of 40°–90°.
The finger (green tip link) is rigidly attached to the coupler, not the output crank — it extends from joint B in the coupler direction (= ground-link direction for a parallelogram). That is what makes the jaw motion parallel.
Workable transmission angle band: typically 40°–140°. Outside this band the four-bar transmits poorly. The defaults sit right at the 40° floor at θ=50° — give yourself more margin when designing your own.
Note for your notebook: the compute_finger_position() function in Section 3 can stay in a local frame with O₂ at the origin (matching Section 1's convention). The starter visualization just adds an offset for clarity — the kinematics math is identical.
